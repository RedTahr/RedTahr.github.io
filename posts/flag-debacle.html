So how would you carry out a Flag Referendum

For starters, one needs an alternative. So who to design that, crowd source it?

How ever one arrives at an alternative flag design or two, it only makes sense (common sense?) that a team comprised of people who design flags and maybe folk who use flags (Captains of the seafaring variety, i.e. a Ships Captain) should be involved, and the last line defense against impractical or unsuitable designs.

Obviously the first line(s) of defence would include filtering out designs of a topical nature or containing brands (sports logos anyone?). As they can and do change far more frequently than a nations flag does.

It's a pity that certain political folk didn't recieve such simple guidance from their counsel before wasting tax payers money on a referendum that narrowly failed in achieving anything. Which I'm glad about as I would have been quite dismayed to become a nation with the current sporting brand being part of the flag. What were they thinking, were they going to call a flag referendum everytime the sports teams redesign their wardrobe?

In case you're missing some context, I'm just voicing my disappointment at the current political leadership of New Zealand.
